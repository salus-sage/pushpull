NOT FOR SALE

In the Name of Science


In exploring the gendered language in botanical sciences, we also encounter silences and omissions.


Science, as a site of knowledge, has been marked by instances of appropriation. It is built upon the labour and traditional ecological wisdom of those who remain anonymous in textbooks, archives, and libraries.


Here we look into complex histories that continue to shape our relationship to plants and flowers and remember the contributions that have been forgotten and invisibilized.


And Still, I Rose


We look towards flowers when the world turns dark. We project our need for hope and healing onto flowers.


An army of guns is met with a single flower held by a protester. It became a way to assert identity and find community when tucked away in a pocket.


Some painted flowers to understand them, while others painted them to find meaning. Some kept them close to demonstrate benevolence, while others kept them far to preserve character. A symbol beyond our comprehension. A symbol that both blurs and defines boundaries. Flowers were here before we arrived and may well be here after we leave.
